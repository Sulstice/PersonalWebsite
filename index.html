SULIMAN SHARIF

Who am I?

Suliman Sharif: San Francisco Native, Mountain Climber, and Boulderer with a ongoing Masters in CS from John Hopkins University, Masters in Organic Chemistry from University of California, Riverside, and undergraduate in Biochemistry from The University of Texas at Austin.

Suliman was an active undergraduate researcher in a variety of labs at the UT Austin: In the Nadasdy Group working on predictive seizure onset based on emotional responses in the insular cortex of the brain, The May Group working on improving the biological activity of bisindole alkaloid anti-malarial drugs, and lastly at the Humphrey Group working on phosphene bidendate ligands for the expansion of metal organic frameworks and new computational prediction methods.

During his undergraduate years he has also completed successful internships at MD Anderson Cancer Center Institute of Applied Cancer Science working on Bromodomain Inhibitors and Macromoltek on Bias Protein Viewers based in JQuery and WebGL. Post graduation he spent a year at the University of California, Riverside at acquiring his Masters at the School of Medicine in the Pellecchia Group doing Rheumatoid Arthritis work and moving on to Computer Science at John Hopkins University in the realm of Computational Biology.

Currently, he works as a Bioinformatics Scientist at Lab7 Systems here in downtown Austin, TX working on data curation.
